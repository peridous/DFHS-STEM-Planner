All throughout this year I have made a consistent effort to meet with and guide kids in elementary to middle school. A core event that I participate in is Book Buddies. Every other late start, I use my mornings to read to a select class at Oak Pointe. This year, I was placed with Mrs. Hartline's kindergarten class. I've met and read with every child in her class. Gradually, I incorporate more challenging and lengthy books, and to my surprise each kid is always up to the challenge. Building vocabulary, speech, and reading skills are all essential to a child’s success in school. Having one on one time with a child can be hard for a teacher with over 20 students, but through Book Buddies I am able to build connections and adjust to the needs of every child. Over time I feel that my communication skills and adaptability have improved vastly. Being flexible and adaptive is important when I work with kindergarteners. Other notable events consist of the STEM trip to Dutch Fork Middle School and the District Magnet Fair. In both of these events, I was able to directly interact with students interested in the STEM program. The events were geared towards middle school students interested in accelerated learning programs like STEM. Through all of this outreach, I’ve developed my speaking skills and I’ve met so many amazing families. Being a part of a child’s education is special and I have the privilege to either simply read a book with them or introduce them to the amazing STEM program. These events have helped me find a passion for working with children and families. I love being able to make a change in my community and the lives of the students I work with, even in small ways.
